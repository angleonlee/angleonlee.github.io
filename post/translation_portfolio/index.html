2020年利兹大学MA Applied Translation Studies英中翻译课考核1（Distinction）

原文：《心不在焉》选自《玉兰树》（The Grandiflora Tree）｜作者：肖纳·科伊（Shonagh Koea）

为了躲避熟人面孔，克莱顿夫人走进了几家咖啡店，但是她却选错了托盘，排错了队伍，给错了零钱，还听错了账单。
店员似乎对她很不耐烦：“你吃东西吗？如果不吃那为什么要拿托盘呢？你想要什么？”
“我不想吃什么，只想要杯咖啡而已。”克莱顿夫人耷拉着脑袋说。
“那你拿着托盘干什么？托盘旁边写着呢，它们只供盛放食物。你没看见么？”
“哦不，我没看见。”克莱顿夫人的头垂得更低了。“要不我这就放回去？我会帮你放回去的，好吗？”她站在那儿，像个老小孩似的，急切地迎合着店员……
咖啡洒了点在杯托里。一天晚上，在克莱顿夫人家中，茶壶咿呀作响，沸腾的水不住地往外冒；整齐有序厨房里，一只窗钩从固定处脱落；这时，十点的钟声刚好敲响。这阵动静吓得克莱顿夫人尖声大叫。固定窗钩螺丝的孔看上去太大了，所以她给每个螺丝缠上了针织线，再把它们小心地安回去。看着螺丝干脆利落地旋进了旧孔洞，克莱顿夫人觉得自己的小妙招还挺管用，一阵意外的愉悦感和成就感涌上了心头。但是，这木工的老手艺究竟是从哪儿来的，自己笨拙的脑袋里怎会有这等知识，她是怎么也回想不起来，怎么也无法琢磨透。她觉得自己像是一名怪盗，从锁住的碗柜中盗取了一笔横财，但转头就把它给忘了。但是她还是暗自窃喜——自己大脑里的某些神秘因子帮助了她独自解决这些难题……
她开始将这些平淡无奇的微小观察看作是人际交往的一部分，将其中不认识的人视作算术中的数字。街上的纷纷扰扰也可以拿来填补平日的宁静。于是，现在的克莱顿夫人享受着收音机里的吵闹，钟爱着车水马龙的喧杂，也沉醉于和陌生人的一举一动。
之后她再次进入家门，便会大喊一声：“你好啊，房子。今天我又和别人交流了呢：见了一些人，看到了一个盲人过马路，和两只狗打了招呼，还有个牙牙学语的两岁小孩向我问了好。我仍在和这个世界保持联系。”
虽然克莱顿夫人知道她不过是在自欺欺人，但她仍注意到了，这间房子静静地听着她那慷慨激昂的每日新闻，太阳也如往日一样照耀着，从窗外投进如朋友微笑一般和煦的阳光。

原文：《转基因生物——敌人？不，更像是个朋友》｜作者：劳拉·克鲁辛（Laura Krusin）｜发布日期：2019年6月19日

谈起转基因生物（genetically modified organisms, GMOs），你的脑海里会浮现怎样的画面？是具有放射性的蜘蛛，某个疯子科学家对着实验室里的化学试剂狞笑，还是可以让你变成“超人”的某种神奇物质？如果你真的这么想，那也不是你的错，大众媒体总想让人们相信这些论调。
但是，我可以告诉你的是，人类至少已经有一万两千年修饰（modify）动植物基因的历史了，不过这些基因修饰（genetic modification）工程并不是在实验室里完成的。玉米（corn）就是一个古老的转基因生物的绝佳例证。我们很熟悉现在金灿灿的甜玉米（sweetcorn），但是它最原始的样子你肯定认不出来。甜玉米的祖先是一个叫做大刍草（Teosintes，又称类蜀黍，原产于美洲）的植物，它长着薄薄的绿色叶片，连一只老鼠都喂不饱，更别说一个成年人了。人类已经有悠久的食用玉米的历史，但是到现在还没有一个产生了超能力（除非漫威系列电影中的英雄都是真实存在的）。我知道你们有疑问：玉米之类的古老谷物在过去是如何进行基因修饰的呢？我们究竟是如何修饰一个生物的基因的呢？看了这篇文章你就能找到答案。
我们现在吃的玉米和橙色的胡萝卜（没错，最初胡萝卜是紫色的而不是橙色）是发生在几千年前的基因修饰的产物，这一过程和现在我们所说的基因修饰很相似，只不过在过去这一过程推进得更加缓慢。每一个植物品种，其个体的培育都是以种植者想得到的性状为导向。你想种出一个橙色而非紫色的胡萝卜？那你就需要把橙色这一性状最突出的胡萝卜放在一起培育，一次又一次地尝试，直到得到你想要的效果。但是如果你想要看到实质性的成效，并且保证某一性状稳定地写入该生物体的遗传密码(genetic code)里，那你就要经历一代又一代的培育。这一过程在本质上和现代基因修饰技术中的过程并无二致，只不过现在我们不再等待动植物代代繁殖，而是通过直接插入所需性状进入生物的基因组（genome）以达到加速的效果。
但是我们为什么要这么煞费苦心地修饰一个物种的基因呢？这是因为通过基因修饰，只需更改遗传物质DNA中的一小段信息，我们就可以显著改变某一物种的特性从而使其更有助于我们的身体健康。
那么转基因作物是好是坏呢？基因修饰可以为植物带来许多好处，比如耐虫害程度提高，所需种植面积减少，甚至营养价值的提升。在进入市场前，这些转基因作物都要通过层层测试，以确保最终到达你盘中的食物是安全的。所以很遗憾地告诉你，短时间内你还不会成为“超人”。人类已经有悠久的基因修饰的历史了，只不过在以前这一过程的速度就像蜗牛爬一样十分缓慢。所以，现代转基因技术只不过是让我们能够加速这一进程，这样我们的粮食生产才能跟得上人口增长的步伐。

汉译英

2020年「完美世界杯」国际口笔译大赛中译英二等奖作品

（原文：《知味便知人生》｜作者：岳强）
Taste Our Multi-flavoured Life
By Yue Qiang
Hu Yang just came back from a long journey and brought with her some chocolate and beef jerky to our salon as a treat for everyone. Xiao Cha reached for a delicately packaged chocolate and unwrapped it. She kept the chocolate in her mouth with an indulging expression on her face. Xiao Cha has a sweet tooth, even a handful of fruit flavoured candies could make her day. Eating something bitter, however, is a “disaster” in Cha’s life because she is way too sensitive to the bitterness. When she was a girl, her mother insisted on feeding her some decoction made from some Chinese medicine. Hardly had she finished the decoction when she started to throw everything up. I once told her one Chinese saying that goes, “good medicine always tastes bitter to the mouth”. She took a glance at me with a pale face, I assumed that her expression, if there is a flavour for it, could taste bitterer than any good medicine in the world.
From the perspective of the dialectic, we can say that both bitterness and sweetness exist in a reconciled world of flavour. It has been scientifically proven that the addition of merely 0.001% of quinine, which tastes bitter, to a 15% sugar solution can make it taste even sweeter. This shows that the sweetness our brain detects comes not only from the sweet-tasting substances, but also the contrast brought by the comparison between the sweet and other flavoured substances. There are many examples of this theory. After we drink the sea water, which is bitter and salty, the purified water can taste much sweeter; the orange that we eat after enjoying a bowl of soup with a robust flavour may taste bitter. A peanut-sized berry known as the “miracle fruit”, which can be found in Hainan Island in Southern China, can turn flavours such as bitterness, astringency, sourness and saltiness into sweetness in five minutes after you eat it. That is because the miracle fruit contains a type of protease which can cause taste changes. The effect of only two micrograms of the protease can last up to three hours.
Even if there is no such a kind of protease available, our palate can still become insensitive to the bitter taste to which we are exposed in a long term. I could tell a bit of a strange taste apart from the bitter sourness at the first time I drank beer. At that time, beers just started to appear on the table of common Chinese households. Some people compared the smell as the “urine of the horse”, an analogy which I did not know was appropriate to make or not. But I am certain that beers are not something that one can sip and savour. Even though people still have the obsession over beers. At that time no bottled beers could be found in the market, instead, people carried their thermal bottles where they bought beers from restaurants. “Tong Yi Xuan” and “Jing Chun” are two restaurants and I am one of their regulars. They were near the Gucheng Subway Station in Beijing. Whichever restaurant I went to, I had to wait in a long line which zigzagged towards the curb outside the restaurant. Normally, one thermal bottle beer was sold along with one jin (500 grams) of Zhutourou (bath chaps). I didn’t understand why people were so obsessed with such a strange-tasting drink at that time. As time went by, I gradually got used to it and then became able to appreciate the charisma the beer had in itself. In this mundane world where people are preoccupied with the hustle and bustle, I come to realise that every bit of loneliness and ups and downs have been condensed into just a bottle of beer.
It is said that we only have four kinds of pure tastes, which are saltiness, sourness, bitterness and sweetness. All other diversified flavours we can feel are various combinations of these four pure tastes. The Site Baijiu (a Chinese liquor made from grains) has a unique flavour which is a combination of the spiciness, bitterness and sourness and the fragrance of grains. Zhou Enlai, the first Premier of China, named the baijiu as “Site” (or “four features”) because of these four distinctive flavours it encompasses. I remember that beers were still a novel drink for the Chinese when I joined the workforce. Those who went out to buy beers with their thermal bottles were usually driven by their curiosity. Most common households had several bottles of the Jiangxi Site Baijiu as their liquor collection. Erguotou (also a Chinese liquor made from grains), though a most well received kind of alcohol, was only served among close family members because it was regarded to be too low-status a drink to be served for guests. Guizhou Maotai and Wuliangye (both are Chinese liquors), though without doubt considered as the most premium ones, are way too expensive to afford for the majority. Among all alcohols above, only the Site Baijiu is both affordable and can make the host look good in front of the guests. Yang Shen, a poet in the Ming dynasty, had this line in his poem that goes “Rejoiced get-together is always accompanied by a warm pot of liquor, around which we chattered and laughed, over the past and the present.” Toasts after toasts, people around the table usually become a bit intoxicated. It is now the time when the taste of the Site Baijiu exceeds more than just four flavours, and the time when they are capable of discovering all the flavours of their lives, in just a few drops of the liquor.
Different flavours can both be combined in various ways and wear each other off. Vinegar can cut the greasiness of the fatty pork and wash the stink of the lamb as well. MSG can relieve the strong taste brought by salt and saccharin; cane sugar can weaken the bitter and sour taste; the list goes on and on and on. The acid-sugar ratio in fruits determines how sweet (or sour) they taste. When I was in primary school, there were events where you had to relive the life of old times to appreciate how “sweet” our present life was in new times. This kind of educational event actually takes the advantage of the “acid-sugar ratio” effect, to put it more clearly, by comparing the sweetness of the new era with the bitterness of the old era. In this way, our expectations of the happiness are tune to a level on which we can appreciate that content is the true happiness.
I can still recall an old movie in which an old man who had seen it all in the world told his younger generation that in his times, people were brought up in adversity, or as his saying goes, “soaked in a pool of bitterness”. And then, he said to them enviously that, in your generation, you were born to taste nothing but sweetness, or as he said, “soaked in a pool of honey”. I am going to recommend the movie to Xiao Cha, whose lifelong dream is to find the pool of honey, jump in and never get out.
